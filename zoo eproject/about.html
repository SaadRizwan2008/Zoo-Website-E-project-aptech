About Central Zoo

Providing a safe haven for wildlife and an educational experience for visitors since 1980.

Our Journey

Explore the rich history and evolution of CENTRAL Zoo over the decades

  • 1980

    Founding Years

    CENTRAL Zoo was established on a modest 10-acre plot of land. The initial collection included local wildlife and a few exotic species.

  • 1990s

    Growth and Development

    Throughout the 1990s, the zoo expanded its collection and improved facilities. Educational programs were introduced to enhance visitor experience.

  • 2000

    Major Expansion

    A significant milestone in our history was the major expansion in 2000, This allowed for larger, the addition of many new species to our collection.

  • 2010s

    Conservation Focus

    In the last decade, CENTRAL Zoo has shifted its focus toward conservation efforts and breeding programs for endangered species.

  • Present Day

    A Modern Wildlife Haven

    Today, CENTRAL Zoo stands as one of the town's most prominent attractions. we house a diverse collection of animals from around the world in environments designed to mimic their natural habitats.

What Sets Us Apart

Discover the special features that make CENTRAL Zoo a unique destination

Spacious Habitats

All our animals enjoy spacious, naturalistic habitats that are carefully designed to meet their physical, psychological, and social needs.

Safety First

We maintain the highest standards of safety for both our animals and visitors, with state-of-the-art barriers and trained staff always on hand.

Nutrition Program

Our dedicated nutrition team ensures that all animals receive proper dietary care with regular monitoring and adjustments as needed.

Educational Programs

We offer a variety of educational programs for visitors of all ages, from school field trips to expert-led workshops and guided tours.

Conservation Efforts

We're actively involved in breeding programs for endangered species and participate in global conservation initiatives.

Expert Staff

Our team includes zoologists, veterinarians, and animal care specialists who are dedicated to the wellbeing of our animal residents.
